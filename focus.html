Before the browser asks

Your camera, your choice.

Focus Sprout needs temporary camera access to estimate posture and whether your head is centered. Access will not be requested until you continue below.

Processed on this device. MediaPipe runs in your browser. Camera frames are never uploaded.

No images are kept. Frames are processed in memory and discarded. There are no screenshots, recordings, logs, or analytics.

You stay in control. Pause or end the session at any time. Either action stops the active camera track.

Privacy and data handling

Nothing to hide.

What the camera does

MediaPipe estimates temporary body and face landmarks in the browser. Focus Sprout immediately reduces those landmarks to a posture deviation angle and a centered/not-centered signal. It does not identify you, recognize faces, or estimate breathing rate.

What is stored or sent

No camera frame, landmark map, biometric template, or recording is written to disk or sent to a server. Derived session values exist only in page memory and vanish when the tab is refreshed or closed. This site has no backend, account system, cookies, analytics, or local storage.

Network activity

The page downloads static Tailwind, MediaPipe, WebAssembly, and model files from jsDelivr and Google storage. These asset requests contain no camera content. A restrictive Content Security Policy limits network connections to those static asset hosts.

How to stop or remove access

Use Pause camera or End session to stop the camera immediately. You can also close the tab or revoke camera access in your browser's site settings. There is no stored study data to delete.

Classroom note

If this is piloted with minors or classmates, explain the camera behavior in advance and get guardian or participant sign-off. This prototype is a focus aid, not a medical, diagnostic, or surveillance tool.
